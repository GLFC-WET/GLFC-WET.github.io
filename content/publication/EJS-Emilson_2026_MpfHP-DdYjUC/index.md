abstract: "Defoliation by insects affects more of the North American boreal forest than wildfire and forest harvest combined, with Eastern Spruce Budworm (Choristoneura fumiferana) as the most prevalent defoliator. Control strategies vary in cost and degree of defoliation suppression, so informed decisions rely on understanding the broader impacts of defoliation. Little is known about how defoliation impacts downstream freshwaters  –  an important gap to address given growing recognition of the need to protect freshwater resources. We use a standardized benthic macroinvertebrate (BMI) bioindicator approach to compare streams in defoliated and undefoliated watersheds. We find dissimilarity in BMI community composition between streams in defoliated and undefoliated watersheds with a resulting influence on regional diversity.
